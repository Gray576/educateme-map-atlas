## Executive Read

The new screenshot pass changed the market picture in three ways:

1. `db=LU` clearly contains a multilingual local market. Looking only through English anchors was distorting the Luxembourg picture, but that does **not** mean France and Germany should be merged into the same local demand layer.
2. Several useful market nodes are not direct competitors but discovery / route platforms:
   - `infpc.lu`
   - `lifelong-learning.lu`
   - `unipop.lu`
   - `moienasbl.lu`
   - `institut-francais-luxembourg.lu`
   - `cfih.lu`
3. We now have at least five distinct market lanes instead of one generic “language training” bucket.

Interpretation rule for this memo:

- `db=LU` = local Luxembourg evidence, even when the query text is in `FR`, `DE`, `LB`, or `EN`
- `db=FR` / `db=DE` = adjacent market evidence, useful for expansion, phrasing, and competitor discovery, but not direct Luxembourg demand proof
