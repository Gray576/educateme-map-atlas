# Post-Step Audit - B5

## Hard Critique

The old card mixed three different things into one story: route compliance, buyer pain, and product packaging. Only the route-compliance layer and the existence of adjacent European analogues survived a strict rerun. The commercial leap from "language and recognition matter" to "agencies need this exact integrated pipeline" is still unproved. The certificate claim was especially weak: Luxembourg proves that some accepted B2 route exists, while Belgium explicitly says a simple course-attendance certificate is not enough. That means the old delivery promise was materially overstated.
