# Keyword Selection Criteria

Database: `LU`

## What to keep for separate follow-up
- Keep keywords that are local to the current database and align with a real product or route.
- Prioritize keywords that appear across multiple domains: they indicate category-level demand, not one-off brand noise.
- Prioritize keywords with at least one of these properties:
  - clear route intent
  - clear commercial intent
  - visible ranking by our domain or close competitors
  - manageable difficulty
  - enough search volume to justify a page or cluster
- Keep branded competitor keywords only when they help understand market structure, switching intent, or page patterns.

## What not to prioritize
- Single-domain noise with no product relevance.
- Purely branded keywords that do not map to our offer or route.
- Zero-volume or n/a variants unless they are critical route terms.
- Adjacent-market phrases that do not belong to the selected database.

## Why we need this
- To identify keywords worth dedicated pages or content clusters.
- To separate market evidence from random keyword expansion.
- To build go-to-market around actual discoverability, not guesses.
- To decide which product cards deserve deeper keyword and SERP work.

## Default ranking heuristic
1. More domains observed.
2. Higher max volume.
3. Better best observed position.
4. Lower difficulty.
5. Stronger route/commercial intent.
