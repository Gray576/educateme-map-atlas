## What survived

1. Luxembourg has a real linguistic-leave route for approved Luxembourgish learning, capped at 200 hours.
2. Luxembourg has a separate company-training co-funding route with 15% base support, a 35% participant-wage uplift in some cases, and examples including language and job-induction training.
3. Biergerpakt is a real public integration programme with life-in-Luxembourg, language, and administrative-procedure modules.
4. Public and private alternatives already exist in Luxembourg, including INLL, Prolingua, and Biergerpakt.
5. The best verified workforce-share number is 45.1%, not the legacy 47%.

## Blocking questions

1. What is the real A6 product boundary: language only, language plus civic integration, or a broader relocation-support bundle?
2. Can any part of A6 qualify under linguistic leave?
3. Can A6 be sold as CVT-co-fundable external training or job-induction training?
4. Does A6 need a partner route for recognized certification or Sproochentest readiness?
5. Are the turnover-reduction and ROI claims defensible at all?
